The Industry Fellow role combines mentorship, technical guidance, and professional engagement. This page outlines what we look for in Fellows and what Fellows receive in return.

## Fellow Profile

<details>
<summary>Technical Expertise</summary>

- Minimum 5 years of professional software development experience
- Strong background in modern development practices and technologies
- Experience with software architecture, design patterns, and best practices
- Familiarity with collaborative development tools and methodologies
- Knowledge of current industry trends and emerging technologies

</details>

<details>
<summary>Leadership and Communication</summary>

- Demonstrated ability to guide and mentor junior developers
- Strong written and verbal communication skills
- Experience working with diverse teams and stakeholders
- Ability to explain complex technical concepts clearly
- Patience and enthusiasm for teaching and knowledge sharing

</details>

<details>
<summary>Commitment and Availability</summary>

- Genuine interest in student development and education
- Ability to commit to multi-semester partnerships
- Flexibility to attend regular in-person events on campus
- Responsiveness to student needs and program requirements
- Alignment with program values and educational mission

</details>

## What Fellows Provide

Industry Fellows commit to meaningful engagement through both in-person and online mentorship activities:

- Attend a minimum of two evening events per semester on SLU campus
- Participate in orientation and mentorship training
- Maintain regular online engagement with matched student teams through check-ins, code reviews, and technical guidance
- Contribute to program development through peer learning sessions and feedback processes

## What Fellows Receive

<details>
<summary>University Affiliation and Benefits</summary>

- University ID cards
- Professional @slu.edu email address
- Library access to research databases and collections
- Co-working space privileges on campus

</details>

<details>
<summary>Professional Development and Recognition</summary>

- Mentorship training that supports leadership and communication skills
- Networking opportunities with industry professionals and university faculty
- Recognition through program certificates, LinkedIn designations, and university-endorsed reference letters
- Personal fulfillment from impacting student success
- Exposure to student projects and academic research
- Continued connections with program graduates

</details>

<details>
<summary>Talent Pipeline Insights</summary>

- Direct exposure to emerging software development talent before they enter the job market
- Firsthand view of how students approach real-world problems, collaborate, and grow
- Relationships with graduates who enter the workforce with proven open source experience
- Early awareness of the skills, tools, and practices the next generation of developers brings

</details>
