Open Source with SLU creates pathways for students at every level to build real skills through meaningful open source work.

## Pre-Collegiate Students

High school students and younger learners can get an early start with open source. Contribute to real projects, learn collaborative development practices, and build a portfolio before you even start college. [Start contributing ⇨](../get_involved/community.md)

## Intercollegiate Students

Not enrolled at SLU? No problem. Students at any college or university can contribute to our open source projects as community members — write code, fix bugs, improve documentation, and collaborate with our teams. [Join our community ⇨](../get_involved/community.md)

## SLU Undergraduates

Turn your required CS capstone into career-launching experience by building production software for real clients, with opportunities to join our paid developer bench. [Gain professional experience ⇨](../get_involved/experience.md)

You can also contribute to projects outside the capstone — pick up issues, build features, and deepen your skills across our portfolio. [Explore our projects ⇨](/projects/portfolio)

## SLU Graduate Students

Lead a capstone software development team as a Tech Lead — coordinate with clients, mentor undergraduates, and serve as maintainer for an open source product. [Become a Tech Lead ⇨](../get_involved/leadership.md)

Graduate students can also mentor developers, contribute to projects, and engage with our research consulting services. [Mentor new developers ⇨](../get_involved/mentoring.md)

## Get Connected
